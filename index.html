Internet History, technology and Security

Created by: University of Michigan

When Late 2012
Total TimeSpent 30 hrs in 2.5 months
Completed
Platform Coursera
Institution Un of Michigan
Homepage Go !
Official description

The impact of technology and networks on our lives, culture, and society continues to increase. The very fact that you can take this course from anywhere in the world requires a technological infrastructure that was designed, engineered, and built over the past sixty years. To function in an information-centric world, we need to understand the workings of network technology. This course will open up the Internet and show you how it was created, who created it and how it works. Along the way we will meet many of the innovators who developed the Internet and Web technologies that we use today.

Why I started this course ?

1) After playing with different online learning platforms (Apple university, Stanford online) without never really engaging with the material, I discoreved Coursera. I wanted to try this new (back in 2012) learning format that looked very engaging. This was my first MOOC enrolement and the first MOOC I've finished.
2) The second reason is because I love the Internet, and I always wanted to know how it came to be.

Key takeaways

With this course, I understood the underlaying history and structures that made/make internet possible. Turing's enigma machines @ Bletchley Park to be the grand parent of computers. Then a network of academics that becomes ARPANET leading Sir Tim Berners-Lee to create the world wide web in CERN. On the "technology" part of the course, four-layer model, HTTP as an application layer, TCP/IP, all those concepts were aligned in order to undertand the foundations of the internet. Lastly, in the "security" I've learned that ...

As my first experience with MOOCs, I was fascinated. Finally a real way to learn online !

Skill improvement

Before After
Internet History Novice: history from highschool lecture. Arpanet becomes the Internet and that is that. Advance beginnner: Can explain how WWII led networked computers to the WWW to the modern internet of applications.
Internet technology Ignorant: TCP/IP is something related to internet. Novice: Overall understanding of internet's 4 layers model (the link, IP, TCP and application layers) for internet data exchange.
Internet security Ignorant: Secure > Unsecure Novice: First approach to digital signatures, SSL, encoding and encryptation.

When Q4 2016 - Q2 2017

6 months
Completed
Platform Udacity
Institution Facebook + Tableau
Homepage Go !
Official description

The Data Analyst Nanodegree program is specifically designed to prepare you for a career in Data Science. As a Data Analyst, you are responsible for obtaining, analyzing, and effectively reporting on data insights ranging from business metrics to user behavior and product performance. This programâ€™s curriculum was developed with leading industry partners to ensure students master the most cutting-edge curriculum. Graduates will emerge fully prepared for this amazing career.

Why I started this course ?

I always was intrigued by information transactions. Since I discovered the internet in 1995, I was hooked by its potential as the information broadcasting network that it is. In the early 2000's, data minning was the edge skill to have, but I lacked the programming skills to enter the environment. Fast forward 10 years, with idustrial engineering studies, 5 years of digital marketing experience and a couple of MOOCs later (ex: PY4E), I wanted to 'enter' the data science ecosytem. During 2016, top influencers [1] [2] started debating about our IA future. I wanted to build an opinion and grow the skills to be an actor in this information rich reality.

Key takeaways

Take aways from this ~ 500 hrs degree are plenty, but there will be divided in 2 sections, a) Data Sciences b) Project based long commitment MOOC's .

  1. Understating of Data Sciences :
    • Different tasks require different roles in the data universe: Data Analyst, Data Engineer and Data Scientist.
    • What's relationship between Artifial Intelligence, Deep learning and Machine Learning, data analysis, business anlaysis and how it relates to business decisions
    • What's a suitable framework in order to apply data analysis to business : 1. Questions, 2. Data wrangling, 3. Data exploration, 4. Conclusions, 5. Vizualisation/Communication
  2. Project based long commitment MOOC's :
    • This is definitely a more enriching way to be immersed in the material (compared to lecture based short commitment courses). Project means having a clear guideline and having to deliver confined by those guidelines. Community discussions, tutoring, multiple submissions and corrections are the best ways of learning.
    • At the end of the course, projects delivered for course completion becomes a portfolio (cf SYLLABUS & PROJECTS section)
    • Delivering a project demand much more commitment and active understanding than watching/reading material.
    • Personnaly, I will challenge myself with more project based online courses from now on pursuing my life long learning journey :)
    • Udacity platform and their "Nanodegree" [1] is a perfect example of this type of proposition.
